## Story Details

**Original headline:** What Are the Most Effective Strategies To Inspire Action on Climate Change?

**Original lede:** Climate change presents a complex global challenge that often leaves individuals uncertain about meaningful action they can take. Researchers at the University of Pennsylvania's Communication Neuroscience Lab are conducting an intervention tournament to test six different strategies designed to shift beliefs and motivate behavioral responses related to climate change.

**Key details:**
- Communication Neuroscience Lab, in partnership with the Annenberg Public Policy Center's Climate Communication Division and the Penn Center for Science, Sustainability and the Media
- 6,000+ participants
- Six strategies tested, including personal reflection exercises and imagination-based approaches
- Outcomes: individual climate actions, community efforts, information sharing, and policy engagement

## Frame Types

**Relevance frames** foreground that the research is *descriptively* connected to the participant's life, community, or people they care about. The participant is an affected party.

**Action frames** foreground that the participant can *do something* in response. The participant has agency.
